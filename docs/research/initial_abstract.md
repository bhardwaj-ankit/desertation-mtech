2. Background

Aircraft maintenance operations generate substantial volumes of operational and maintenance-related information through electronic Technical Logbooks (eTechlogs), maintenance records, engineering orders, and operational advisories. These records are typically distributed across multiple systems and consist largely of unstructured or semi-structured textual information.

In airline maintenance operations, engineers frequently analyze recurring defects, maintenance history, Minimum Equipment List (MEL) references, troubleshooting procedures, and operational advisories under strict operational timelines. Existing workflows are heavily dependent on manual interpretation, prior experience, and fragmented information retrieval mechanisms, which can increase cognitive workload and response times.

The current work environment within the airline industry increasingly emphasizes:

predictive maintenance,
operational reliability,
AI-assisted troubleshooting,
safety compliance,
and intelligent decision-support systems.

The proposed dissertation is highly relevant to the current organizational environment because it aims to design an intelligent AI-based maintenance copilot capable of:

retrieving evidence-grounded maintenance information,
assisting in defect triage,
identifying recurring defect patterns,
providing explainable recommendations,
and supporting maintenance engineers with contextual operational intelligence.

The project aligns with ongoing industry trends involving digital transformation, AI adoption in aviation maintenance, and intelligent operational analytics.

3. Objectives

The primary objectives of the proposed dissertation are as follows:

To study existing AI-based approaches for aircraft maintenance intelligence, conversational systems, predictive maintenance, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).
To design and develop an Agentic Multimodal AI Framework capable of analyzing aircraft techlog data and maintenance-related information.
To implement a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) based conversational assistant capable of retrieving and citing evidence from maintenance-related knowledge sources.
To develop predictive models capable of identifying recurring defect patterns and generating early-warning indicators using structured and unstructured maintenance data.
To design safety-constrained decision-support mechanisms with confidence scoring, abstention logic, and audit logging.
To evaluate the effectiveness of multimodal AI techniques combining textual defect narratives, structured metadata, and simulated operational signals.
To study the feasibility of integrating explainable and evidence-grounded AI systems within aviation maintenance workflows.
To analyze system performance using metrics such as retrieval accuracy, hallucination reduction, defect prediction performance, response latency, and recommendation quality.
