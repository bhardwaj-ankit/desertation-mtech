## 1. Broad Area of Work

Artificial Intelligence is transforming aviation maintenance and operational reliability through intelligent automation, predictive analytics, and decision-support systems. Aircraft Technical Logbooks (Techlogs) contain large volumes of unstructured defect narratives, maintenance actions, and operational observations which are critical for troubleshooting and maintenance decision-making. However, extracting actionable intelligence from these heterogeneous data sources remains a significant challenge.

The proposed dissertation focuses on developing an **Agentic Multimodal AI Framework** for aircraft techlog intelligence by integrating:

- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Predictive Analytics
- Multimodal Learning
- Conversational AI
- Safety-Constrained Decision Support
- Explainable AI and Auditability

The framework aims to support aircraft maintenance engineers and operational teams by providing evidence-grounded recommendations, intelligent defect triage, recurring defect prediction, and explainable maintenance insights.

The research project covers the following academic and technical domains:

- Natural Language Processing (NLP)
- Conversational AI
- Deep Learning
- Multimodal AI Systems
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
- Predictive Modeling
- Reinforcement Learning Concepts
- Graph-based Intelligence
- AI Safety and Explainability
- ML System Optimization

## 2. Background

Aircraft maintenance operations generate substantial volumes of operational and maintenance-related information through electronic Technical Logbooks (eTechlogs), maintenance records, engineering orders, and operational advisories. These records are typically distributed across multiple systems and consist largely of unstructured or semi-structured textual information.

In airline maintenance operations, engineers frequently analyze recurring defects, maintenance history, Minimum Equipment List (MEL) references, troubleshooting procedures, and operational advisories under strict operational timelines. Existing workflows are heavily dependent on manual interpretation, prior experience, and fragmented information retrieval mechanisms, which can increase cognitive workload and response times.

The current work environment within the airline industry increasingly emphasizes:

- Predictive maintenance
- Operational reliability
- AI-assisted troubleshooting
- Safety compliance
- Intelligent decision-support systems

The proposed dissertation is highly relevant to the current organizational environment because it aims to design an intelligent AI-based maintenance copilot capable of:

- Retrieving evidence-grounded maintenance information
- Assisting in defect triage
- Identifying recurring defect patterns
- Providing explainable recommendations
- Supporting maintenance engineers with contextual operational intelligence

The project aligns with ongoing industry trends involving digital transformation, AI adoption in aviation maintenance, and intelligent operational analytics.

## 3. Objectives

The primary objectives of the proposed dissertation are as follows:

1. To study existing AI-based approaches for aircraft maintenance intelligence, conversational systems, predictive maintenance, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG).
2. To design and develop an Agentic Multimodal AI Framework capable of analyzing aircraft techlog data and maintenance-related information through multiple specialized AI agents.
3. To implement a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) based conversational assistant (Techlog Copilot) capable of retrieving and citing evidence from maintenance knowledge sources including MEL/CDL extracts, manuals, engineering orders, and prior techlog cases.
4. To develop predictive models capable of identifying recurring defect patterns and generating early-warning indicators using fused textual defect narratives and simulated operational health signals.
5. To design safety-constrained decision-support mechanisms incorporating confidence scoring, abstention logic, escalation handling, and audit logging aligned to aviation AI governance standards.
6. To evaluate the effectiveness of multimodal AI techniques combining textual defect narratives, structured maintenance metadata, and simulated operational signals against single-modality baselines.
7. To study the feasibility of integrating explainable and evidence-grounded AI systems within aviation maintenance workflows in compliance with EASA Level 1/Level 2 AI guidance.
8. To analyze system performance using metrics such as retrieval accuracy (Recall@k, nDCG), hallucination rate reduction, defect prediction performance (AUROC, F1), response latency (p95), recommendation quality, and audit completeness.
